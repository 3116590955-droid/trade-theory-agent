Comparative Advantage in Multi-dimensional Ricardian Models
Difficulty: How to construct such chains with N goods and J countries?  Potential Solutions:
вҖў Jones (1961): Look at environments in which each country produces only one good
вҖў Wilson (1980): No need to predict pattern of trade, can determine comparative statics such as 
changes in prices and welfare without it.
вҖў Costinot (2009): Restrict attention to environments in which unit labor costs are log-submodular 
in good characteristics and country characteristics, so comparative advantage chains can be 
constructed
вҖў Eaton-Kortum (2002): Assume productivities are drawn from a Frechet distribution.  Like Wilson, 
doesnвҖҷt predict pattern of trade for individual goods, but can predict aggregate trade flows and 
comparative statics much simpler and better suited for empirical work.
