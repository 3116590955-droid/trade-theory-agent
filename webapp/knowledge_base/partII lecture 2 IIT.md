Overview Intra Industry Trade
In a neoclassical world, differences in relative autarky prices– due to differences in technology, factor endowments, or preferences– are the only rationale for trade.
This suggests that:
“Different” countries should trade more.
“Different” countries should specialize in “different” goods.
In the real world, however, we observe that:
The bulk of world trade is between “similar” countries.
These countries tend to trade “similar” goods.

“New” Trade Theory proposes IRS as an alternative rationale for international trade and a potential explanation for the previous facts.
Basic idea:
Because of IRS, similar countries will specialize in different goods to take advantage of large-scale production, thereby leading to trade.
Because of IRS, countries may exchange goods with similar factor content.
In addition, IRS may provide new source of gains from trade if it induces firms to move down their average cost curves.

Intra Industry Trade - Types
Horizontal intra-industry trade(HIIT)
the simultaneous exports and imports of goods classified in the same sector and at the same stage of processing; or
horizontally differentiated products (products differentiated by attributes)
Vertical intra-industry trade(VIIT)
the simultaneous exports and imports of goods classified in the same sector but at different stages of processing; or
vertically differentiated products (products differentiated by quality)

Dixit-Stiglitz (DS) monopolistic competition
To explain intra industry trade flows, Paul Krugman realized that the exported goods are usually similar, but not identical to, the imported goods
Example: China exports Huawei, Xiaomi, Oppo smartphones and imports Samsung, Apple smartphones; these goods are produced with similar technology and factor intensity, but are not the same
Arguing that consumers like to demand different varieties, Krugman builds on the Dixit-Stiglitz (DS) monopolistic competition model to explain intra industry trade

Dixit-Stiglitz and love-of-variety
DS utility is characterized by love-of-variety; example take  = 0.5
Suppose N = 1, I = 8, p1 = 1
Consumers then buy 8/1 = 8 units of the single good and U1 = 8
Now suppose there are two varieties (N = 2)
There are no other changes: income   I = 8 and both firms charge the same price (p1 = p2 = 1)
Obviously, consumers will buy 4 units of both varieties
This implies that utility rises to U2 = 16
Conclusion: N↑ implies U↑, there is love-of-variety

Krugman final goods varieties intra industry trade
Krugman’s explanation of intra industry trade is now simple, based on final goods
Suppose Belgium has 5 million workers in the DS model and produces (and consumes) 10,000 varieties in autarky
Suppose the Netherlands has 7 million workers; technology is the same; remember N is proportional to market size
So the Netherlands produces and consumes 7/5  10,000 = 14,000 varieties
This implies that in autarky Dutch workers achieve a higher welfare level than Belgian workers (market size is important)

Krugman final goods varieties intra industry trade
If the two countries start to trade production levels and prices do not change
However, consumers in both countries have access to all varieties
Since they love variety, they consume all goods available on the market
The Dutch import part of production of Belgian varieties
And pay for it by exporting part of production of Dutch varieties
This leads to two-way-trade in similar products (thus intra industry trade)
Trade increases the extent of the market, which leads to variety gains from trade

Allow relative factor-abundance differences between the nations and add a second sector, which is L-intense to manufactures, which is K-intense.
We get a hybrid of the HO model and the Krugman model
This model is often called the Helpman-Krugman model.
Netting out intra-industry trade (i.e. only looking at a nations exports of manufactures minus its imports of manufactures), the trade pattern follows the HO Thm, i.e. L-rich nations export L-intense goods.
Plus we have IIT in manufactures.
Thus we get both intra-industry and inter-industry trade.
As nations’ relative endowments become more similar (e.g. US and EU) intra-industry trade is more important than for dissimilar nations (EU and Africa, e.g.).

Concluding remarks
Trade flows are intensive between similar countries (similar technology and factor abundance)
This involves two-way trade in similar goods (intra industry)
Intra industry trade is measured using the Grubel-Lloyd index
Product variety and fragmentation are crucial for understanding intra industry trade (IIT)
Number of goods available is limited by economies of scale市场很小，为了满足规模经济，只能生产很少几种商品，且每种产量巨大。消费者的选择非常有限（比如只有几种颜色的衣服可选）
This provides firms market power; as modeled in Dixit-Stiglitz monopolistic competition model (constant mark-up)
Trade in final goods raises extent of the market; leads to love-of-variety gains from trade (Krugman; mainly horizontal IIT)
Trade in intermediate goods raises extent of the market; leads to production externality gains from trade (Ethier; mainly vertical IIT, fragmentation)
